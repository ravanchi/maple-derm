Skin Cancer

Our dermatologists provide comprehensive diagnosis and treatment for various types of skin cancer. The process typically begins with a careful biopsy of suspicious lesions, followed by appropriate treatment options. While many cases can be fully addressed at our clinic, complex cases may involve coordination with specialized skin cancer surgeons or plastic surgeons to ensure optimal outcomes tailored to each patient's specific condition.

Below are the common types of skin cancer we treat:

Basal Cell Carcinoma (BCC)

The most common type of skin cancer. Typically appears as a pearly or waxy bump, or a flat, flesh-colored lesion on sun-exposed areas.

Squamous Cell Carcinoma (SCC)

The second most common form of skin cancer, often appearing as a firm red nodule or a flat lesion with a scaly, crusted surface.

Melanoma

A serious form of skin cancer that develops in cells that produce melanin. Early detection is crucial for successful treatment.
